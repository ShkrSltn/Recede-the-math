Primary School Math Cheatsheet

Arithmetic Operations

  • Addition: Combining quantities. Example: If John has 3 apples and Mary gives him 2 more, how many apples does John have in total? Answer: 3+2=5.
  • Subtraction: Finding the difference between quantities. Example: If there are 8 candies in a jar and 3 candies are taken out, how many candies are left? Answer: 8−3=5.
  • Multiplication: Repeated addition. Example: If there are 4 rows of desks in a classroom, and each row has 6 desks, how many desks are there in total? Answer: 4×6=24.
  • Division: Sharing or grouping into equal parts. Example: If there are 12 cookies to be shared equally among 4 children, how many cookies will each child get? Answer: 12÷4=3 cookies per child.

Fractions

  • Numerator/Denominator: The numerator represents the number of equal parts being considered, while the denominator represents the total number of parts in the whole. Example: In 2/5, 2 is the numerator and 5 is the denominator.
  • Adding/Subtracting Fractions: Example: 1/4 + 1/4 = 2/4 = 1/2.
  • Multiplying Fractions: Example: 2/3 × 3/5 = 6/15 = 2/5.
  • Dividing Fractions: Example: 3/4 ÷ 1/2 = 3/4 × 2/1 = 6/4 = 3/2.

Decimals

  • Place Value: Each digit's position has a value based on powers of 10. Example: In 3.75, the 7 is in the tenths place, representing 7/10.
  • Adding/Subtracting Decimals: Example: 3.25 + 1.6 = 4.85.
  • Multiplying Decimals: Example: 2.5 × 0.4 = 1.0.
  • Dividing Decimals: Example: 4.8 ÷ 0.6 = 8.0.

Integers

  • Adding/Subtracting Integers: Example: 5 + (-3) = 2, 10 - (-4) = 14.
  • Multiplying/Dividing Integers: Example: 3 × (-2) = -6, 12 ÷ (-3) = -4.

Algebra

  • Variables: Letters or symbols representing unknown or changing values. Example: Solve for x in 2x + 3 = 9.
  • Expressions: Mathematical phrases containing numbers, variables, and operations. Example: 3x - 2.
  • Equations: Mathematical sentences stating two expressions are equal. Example: 2x + 5 = 11.
  • Solving Equations: Example: 3x - 7 = 8 → 3x = 15 → x = 5.

Geometry

  • Perimeter: Example: Perimeter of a rectangle with length 5 and width 3 is P = 2(5 + 3) = 16.
  • Area: Example: Area of a rectangle with length 4 and width 2 is A = 4 × 2 = 8.
  • Volume: Example: Volume of a cube with side length 3 is V = 3^3 = 27.

Ratios and Proportions

  • Ratio: Example: Ratio of boys to girls in a class is 3:2.
  • Proportion: Example: If 25/x = 10/52, then x = 4.

Statistics and Probability

  • Mean: Example: Mean of 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 is (3 + 4 + 5 + 6 + 7)/5 = 5.
  • Median: Example: Median of 2, 4, 6, 8, 10 is 6.
  • Mode: Example: Mode of 1, 2, 2, 3, 4, 4, 4, 5 is 4.
  • Probability: Example: Probability of rolling a 3 on a fair six-sided die is 1/6.
